Let's start our questionnaire by talking about architecture.
Are there any specific design styles from different eras or cultures that inspire you?
Ancient Egyptian architecture: characterized by massive stone structures, such as pyramids and temples, with a focus on symmetry, balance, and grandeur.
Classical Greek architecture: characterized by the use of columns, pediments, and arches, with a focus on proportion, symmetry, and balance.
Byzantine: this style of architecture emerged in the Eastern Roman Empire in the 5th century and is characterized by the use of domes, mosaic flooring
Gothic architecture: characterized by the use of pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses, with a focus on height, light, and intricate ornamentation.
Renaissance architecture: characterized by the revival of classical forms and motifs, such as columns, pediments, and domes, with a focus on proportion, harmony, and perspective.
Baroque architecture: characterized by grandeur, drama, and ornamentation, with a focus on the use of light and shadow to create a sense of movement and depth.
Art Nouveau architecture: characterized by sinuous curves, organic forms, and intricate ornamentation, with a focus on the integration of art and nature.
Art Deco: This style of architecture emerged in the 1920s and 1930s and is characterized by geometric shapes, bold colors, and streamlined designs.
Bauhaus: This style of architecture emerged in Germany in the 1920s and is characterized by simplicity, functionality, and the use of industrial materials such as steel and concrete.
Modernist architecture: characterized by clean lines, simple forms, and the use of new materials and construction techniques, with a focus on function and efficiency.
Postmodern architecture: characterized by a playful, eclectic, and often ironic approach to design. This style of architecture emerged in the late 20th century.
Islamic architecture: This style of architecture is characterized by the use of intricate geometric patterns, calligraphy, and the use of courtyards
Japanese architecture: This style of architecture emphasizes the use of natural materials such as wood and stone and is characterized by simplicity, harmony, and a connection to nature.
Nordic architecture: This style of architecture is characterized by its connection to nature, emphasis on sustainability, and minimalism, while incorporating both traditional and modern elements.
